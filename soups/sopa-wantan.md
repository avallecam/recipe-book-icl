# Sopa Wantán (Peruvian Wonton Soup)


## Ingredients

- **200 g** ground pork (or chicken)
- **1 pack** wonton wrappers
- **2 tablespoons** soy sauce
- **1 tablespoon** oyster sauce
- **1 teaspoon** sesame oil
- **1** spring onion (green onion), finely chopped
- **1 teaspoon** grated ginger
- **2** garlic cloves, minced
- **6 cups** (about 1.4 L) chicken broth
- **1 tablespoon** vegetable oil
- **2** eggs
- **1 cup** Chinese cabbage (or napa cabbage), chopped
- **Salt and white pepper** to taste
- **1–2 tablespoons** soy sauce (extra, for seasoning broth)
- **Fresh coriander (cilantro)** chopped, for garnish
- **Chili sauce or ají** (optional, for serving)


## Instructions

1. **Prepare the Filling:**
   - In a bowl, combine the ground pork, soy sauce, oyster sauce, sesame oil,
     chopped spring onion, ginger, and minced garlic. Mix well until fully
     incorporated.

2. **Assemble the Wantanes:**
   - Place a small teaspoon of filling in the center of each wonton wrapper.
     Moisten the edges with water, fold into a triangle, and seal well. Bring
     the corners together to form the classic wonton shape. Repeat until all
     filling is used.

3. **Cook the Wantanes:**
   - Bring a pot of water to a boil. Add the wontons in batches and cook for
     about 3–4 minutes, or until they float and the filling is cooked through.
     Remove and set aside.

4. **Prepare the Broth:**
   - In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add a bit of
     minced garlic and ginger (optional extra for flavor) and sauté briefly.
     Pour in the chicken broth and bring to a gentle simmer.

5. **Add the Eggs:**
   - Lightly beat the eggs and slowly pour them into the simmering broth while
     stirring gently to create egg ribbons.

6. **Add Vegetables and Seasoning:**
   - Add the chopped cabbage to the broth and cook for 2–3 minutes until just
     tender. Season with salt, white pepper, and additional soy sauce to taste.

7. **Serve:**
   - Place several cooked wontons in each bowl. Ladle the hot broth over them,
     ensuring some cabbage and egg ribbons are included. Garnish with chopped
     coriander and serve with chili sauce or ají on the side.
